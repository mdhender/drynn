# Vacation Mode

**STATUS: DRAFT.** The self-service flow described here is planned, not yet implemented. This document describes the intended player experience.

A short note for players who need to step away from a drynn game without losing their empire.

## The Setup

Drynn runs on a turn cadence. Each turn you are expected to submit orders for your empire. If you skip a turn, your empire creates no orders and is mechanically processed for production and attrition — food, fuel, and morale drift over time without direction.

Drynn does not model vacation as a game concept. Instead, you assign an agent to run your empire while you are away. The game engine does not distinguish "on vacation" from "using an agent"; there is only who is submitting orders this turn.

## Before You Leave

1. Open your player profile.
2. Pick an agent from the list. Typical options:
   - **Vacation Agent** — repeats your last submitted orders each turn.
   - **Sneaky Agent** — plays Machiavellian tactics.
   - Others may be available.
3. Save.

The agent will submit orders on your behalf until you clear it.

## When You Come Back

1. Open your player profile.
2. Clear the agent selection.
3. Save.

You are back in control for the next turn.

## If the Selection Is Locked

If the GM has assigned an agent to your empire — for example, to hold your seat through an unplanned absence — the selection is locked to you. Talk to the GM if you need to change or remove it.

## Why It Works This Way

Life outside the game happens. Vacation mode is an out-of-game affordance, not a game rule: drynn does not track vacation as a status or a reason. The record that says who controls your empire simply has an agent assigned for a while, and the agent is removed when you return.
